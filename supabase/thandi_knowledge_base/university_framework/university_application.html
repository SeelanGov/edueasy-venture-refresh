Apply to University with EduEasy

Ready to study at a South African university? EduEasy and Thandi make applying easy, whether through the Central Applications Office (CAO) or directly to universities. Calculate your APS, track applications, and get funding tips—all in one place!

CAO vs. Direct Applications

EduEasy guides you through both options:

  • CAO: Apply to multiple KwaZulu-Natal universities (e.g., UKZN, DUT) with one form for ~R250.
  • Direct: Apply to universities like UCT or Wits via their portals. EduEasy tracks all your applications for free.

Admission Point Score (APS)

Your APS uses Matric results to check eligibility. Each subject mark gives points:

Mark Range APS Points
80–100% 7
70–79% 6
60–69% 5
50–59% 4
40–49% 3

Example: Medicine at UCT needs an APS of 38+ with Maths and Science at 70%+. Use Thandi to calculate your APS for free!

How to Apply with EduEasy

Follow these steps with Thandi’s help:

  1. Choose Programs: Pick up to 6 programs via CAO or university portals. Thandi can suggest programs based on your Matric subjects.
  2. Gather Documents: Upload certified ID, Matric results, and proof of payment to EduEasy’s secure platform.
  3. Apply Online: Use CAO or university sites (e.g., UCT). EduEasy syncs your applications.
  4. Pay Fees: CAO (~R250) or university-specific fees. Check waivers with Thandi.
  5. Track with EduEasy: Monitor all applications in one place. Upgrade to EduEasy Premium for priority tracking and reminders.

Key Deadlines

  • CAO: Opens May 2025, closes September 2025 (check CAO).
  • Direct: Varies (e.g., UCT: July 31, 2025; Wits: September 30, 2025).

Tips for Success

  • Start Early: Apply with Grade 11 results to beat deadlines.
  • Check APS: Use EduEasy’s free APS calculator to match programs.
  • Secure Funding: Apply for NSFAS via Thandi’s guidance after submitting applications.
