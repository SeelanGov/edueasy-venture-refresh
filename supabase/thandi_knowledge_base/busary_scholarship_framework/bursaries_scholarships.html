What Are Bursaries and Scholarships?

Bursaries cover tuition, accommodation, or books, often requiring work-back agreements. Scholarships reward academic or other achievements and may not require repayment.

Popular Funding Options

Here are key bursaries and scholarships:

Funding Type Eligibility Source
NSFAS Bursary Household income < R350,000/year, SA citizen NSFAS
Funza Lushaka Bursary Education students, commit to teaching DHET

Eligibility

  • Academic: Most require strong Matric results (e.g., 60%+ for NSFAS, 70%+ for scholarships).
  • Financial Need: Bursaries like NSFAS need proof of income.
